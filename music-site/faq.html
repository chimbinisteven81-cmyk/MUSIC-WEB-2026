Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SoundWave. Can't find your answer? Contact us.

🔍
Is SoundWave really free to use? ▼

Yes, 100% free for listeners. You can stream and download any track on SoundWave without paying anything, ever. No hidden fees, no premium tier for listeners.

Do I need an account to listen? ▼

No account needed to browse and stream music. However, creating a free account lets you save favorites, create playlists, and follow artists. Sign up takes less than 30 seconds.

Can I download tracks for offline listening? ▼

Yes! Every track on SoundWave is available for free download. Artists choose to make their music freely downloadable as part of our platform's open access philosophy.

What audio quality is available? ▼

Streaming quality depends on the artist's upload. Most tracks are available at 320kbps MP3 or lossless FLAC. You can see the quality indicator on each track's player page.

How do I create a playlist? ▼

Create a free account, then click the "+ Playlist" button on any track's player page, or go to your Profile Dashboard and click "New Playlist" in the Playlists tab.

Is there a mobile app? ▼

Our mobile app is coming soon for iOS and Android. In the meantime, our website is fully responsive and works great on any mobile browser. Add it to your home screen for an app-like experience.

Why do artists pay to upload? ▼

Upload fees fund our free streaming infrastructure and ensure quality content. This model lets us keep listening completely free while giving artists a professional platform with real analytics and global reach.

What formats can I upload? ▼

We accept MP3, WAV, FLAC, and AAC audio files up to 200MB per track. For cover art, we accept JPG and PNG at a minimum of 500×500 pixels. We recommend 3000×3000px for best quality.

Do I keep ownership of my music? ▼

Absolutely. You retain 100% ownership and copyright of all music you upload. SoundWave is a distribution platform, not a label. You can remove your music at any time.

How do I get analytics for my tracks? ▼

Basic analytics (play counts, downloads) are available on all plans. Advanced analytics (listener demographics, geographic data, playlist adds) are available on Monthly and Annual Pro plans.

Can labels upload on behalf of artists? ▼

Yes. Our Annual Pro plan supports team accounts, allowing labels and management companies to manage multiple artists from one account. Contact artist relations for enterprise pricing.

What payment methods do you accept? ▼

We accept all major credit and debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex), PayPal, and Apple Pay. All payments are processed securely through Stripe.

Can I cancel my subscription anytime? ▼

Yes, cancel anytime from your account settings. Your plan stays active until the end of the billing period. No cancellation fees, no questions asked.

Is there a free trial? ▼

Monthly and Annual Pro plans include a 7-day free trial. You won't be charged until the trial ends, and you can cancel before then with no charge.

What happens to my music if I cancel? ▼

If you cancel a subscription, tracks uploaded during your active subscription remain live. If you need to upload new tracks, you'll need to resubscribe or purchase a single-track upload.

Do you offer refunds? ▼

We offer full refunds within 7 days of purchase if you haven't uploaded any tracks. For single-track purchases, refunds are available within 24 hours if the track hasn't been published yet.

What content is not allowed? ▼

We don't allow content that infringes copyright, contains hate speech, promotes violence, or violates our community guidelines. All uploads are reviewed before going live. See our full Terms of Service.

How do I file a copyright claim? ▼

Email legal@soundwave.com with your DMCA takedown notice. We respond to all valid claims within 48 hours. Include the track URL, your ownership proof, and contact information.

Can I use SoundWave music in my videos? ▼

Usage rights depend on the individual artist's license. Look for the license badge on each track. Many artists offer Creative Commons licenses. Always check before using music commercially.

Still have questions?

Our support team is ready to help you out.
